# Capstone\_1



**Data Analytics Capstone - Territory 10 Sales (Jim Heck - West Region)**



​**About My Project**



​For my Capstone project, I worked on a sales database to help a sales manager understand how their territory is doing. My task was to focus on Territory 10. I had to use SQL to find

the data and then use Excel to show my findings in a way that is easy to understand.



​**What I Did**



​Database Setup - I worked with a database that has 8 different tables. I had to make sure the tables were connected correctly so I could pull the right information.



​SQL Queries - I wrote a SQL script to calculate the Total Online Sales for every state in my territory. This helped me see the exact dollar amounts for each location.



​Excel - I exported my query results from MySQL Workbench as a CSV file and then imported that data into Excel to build my dashboard. I created a pivotable and a PivotChart to visualize the sales. I sorted the chart from largest to smallest.



**​What I Found (Results)**



​After looking at my chart, I found some important information



​California is the best state in Territory 10, with the highest sales ($14.7M).



​Washington and Oregon are also doing very well.



​Some states like Minnesota have much lower sales, so we might need to look into why that is happening.





**My recommendation for the next quarter is focused on three main things I found in the data**



&#x20;​1. Keep California as the Priority the results showed California is our #1 state for online sales, we should focus our biggest marketing efforts there. It's our most reliable source of     income.



​2. Promote Technology Products My analysis for Question 4 showed that Technology products have the highest average sales. I recommend we create a special promotion for these items to increase our total revenue even more.



3\. Support the West Team I noticed that the other managers in the West aren't showing any sales yet. They are labeled as online sales without a specific territory. I recommend assigning    them specific states to manage, just like Jim Heck has Colorado. My final recommendation is to share Jim Heck's successful online strategies with the rest of the West team so the whole region can grow.
